Health System Overview

Gray Zone Warfare uses body-part simulation — no traditional hit points. Understanding these systems helps you treat the right problem fast.

Health Panel Indicators

Wounds

Red markers from projectile or melee hits that bypass protective gear. Categorized as Light, Medium, or Severe. Light wounds heal over time — Medium and Severe require bandages.

Bruises

Purple markers when projectiles are stopped by protective gear, causing pain. Heal naturally over time — no treatment required.

Treated Wounds

Green markers after Medium or Severe wounds are bandaged. Cause temporary discomfort before healing completely on their own.

Organ Damage

Brain, heart, lungs, and liver can be damaged. Destruction of the heart or brain is instantly fatal. All organ damage is treated with a surgery kit.

Bone Damage

Arm and leg bones can be damaged by impacts or falls. Causes debuffs to the corresponding stamina and movement. Repair with a splint.

Health Factors

Blood

Blood loss causes progressively worse vision effects and leads to coma if severe. Blood regenerates naturally — but only when hydration and energy are both above 50%.

Intoxication

Builds up with stimulant use. Gradually decreases on its own or can be cleared faster with the appropriate pills.

Radiation

Accumulates in irradiated zones over time. Must be treated with radiation pills — reaching lethal levels causes death.

Stamina Systems

Leg Stamina

Drains when sprinting, jumping, vaulting, or wading. Fully depleted blocks sprinting, jumping, and vaulting. Heavier carry weight drains it faster. Leg injuries lower the maximum capacity.

Arm Stamina

Drains while aiming, firing, using melee, or vaulting. Unlike legs, full depletion does not block actions — it applies a weapon stability penalty instead. Heavier weapons drain it faster.

Breath Meter

A hidden meter that drains when holding breath, sprinting hard, or when lungs are damaged. When depleted, holding breath for precision aiming is not possible.

Treatment Quick Reference

What each medical item type treats — and what it does not.

Bandages

Treat: Medium and Severe wounds.

Don't treat: Bruises, bones, or organs.

Light wounds heal over time — bandages are not always needed for them.

Tourniquets

Treat: Active bleeding — stops blood drain immediately.

Don't treat: The underlying wounds (still need bandages after).

Apply first, treat wounds second. Always carry at least two.

Splints

Treat: Damaged and destroyed arm and leg bones.

Don't treat: Organ damage, wounds, or bleeding.

Required to cure Limping and restore lost stamina capacity from bone injuries.

Surgery Kit

Treat: Damaged and destroyed organs — brain, heart, lungs, liver.

Don't treat: Wounds, bleeding, or bones.

"When in doubt, SurKit it out." Most complex symptoms trace back to organ damage.

Blood Bag

Treat: Low blood — directly replenishes your blood pool.

Don't treat: Active bleeding (stop the bleed first).

Always stop bleeding before using a blood bag or the blood will drain again.

Pills

Treat: Pain, intoxication, or radiation — depending on pill type.

Don't treat: Structural damage such as organs or bones.

Read the item description — each pill type is specialized for one purpose.

Stimulators

Effect: Alter health system behavior with various buffs and drawbacks.

Note: Some stimulators cause intoxication buildup.

Effects are detailed in the item description. Use with awareness of side effects.
